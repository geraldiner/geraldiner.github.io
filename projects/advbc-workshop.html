Advanced Breast Cancer Workshop

The Advanced Stage Breast Cancer Workshop (ABC Workshop) is a live event turned virtual due to COVID. Normally a 3-day conference, this 1-day workshop focuses specifically on the phenomena around advanced stages of breast cancer, including risk factors, prevalence, intervention strategies, and novel ways to apply artificial intelligence and machine learning.

Problem

Designing a unique virtual conference experience in Gather.town for researchers in different timezones with different backgrounds proved to be a complex challenge.

Dr. Shepherd was sold on Gather.town with it‚Äôs game-style mechanics, where attendees could move around a virtual space with their arrow keys, just like at a real conference.

However, knowing that our target audience was older and less likely to buy in to the high interactivity, we had to figure out a way that was accessible to everyone interested in attending.

Process

Two weeks from initial planning meeting to ideas for implementation and copywriting

Our goal was to form a deep understanding of the attendee journey to help inform design decisions that welcome and guide attendees rather than frustrate and turn away.

Understand the Experience

Pulling on previous experience and other research, we started with understanding the key points of an attendee's experience at a conference.

Empathize with the Attendee

Empathizing with the attendee helped us stay focused on their needs and avoid letting our desire for features take precedence.

Generate Ideas for Implementation

From the previous work, we were able to map out the whole experience and generate ideas for how to address anticipated issues at each step.

Mike Morrison's work led me to more research from the Nielsen Norman Group about best practices for legibility, readability, and ‚Äúinformation scent‚Äù to be implemented in our copywriting.

Key Takeaways

Provide benefits to users by making the content easy to read and easy to understand.

Guide viewers around the site with the ‚Äúinformation scent‚Äù by providing clear labels and context, so they can feel confident clicking on links that bring them closer to what they‚Äôre looking for.

Know your audience so you can choose words familiar to them. Be succinct, aim for a 10-12th grade reading level, and follow web formatting-rules for readability.

Storyboard

A storyboard helped me identify key moments in the experience of an attendee from first awareness to after the event.

This kept the focus on the attendee's point-of-view rather than our own

The storyboard revealed issues and key points of the experience early in the planning process.

The ABC Workshop website would be a main source of information for potential and registered attendees.

Keeping the takeaways from the research in mind, it should be designed in a way that helps viewers find the information they‚Äôre looking for as quickly as possible.

The experience around Gather.town is a whole other experience to design for.

Knowing that this might be new for a lot of attendees, there should be guidance for them, without overwhelming or frustrating them.

Journey Map

Expanding on the storyboard, I looked at the whole attendee journey to outline the experience and come up with ideas for implementation.

I identified the key tasks at each stage as well as their emotional state.

See the full ABC Workshop Attendee Journey Map

For each stage of the attendee journey, I identified the:

  1. Goals: The main accomplishments at that point of the journey
  2. Thoughts: Questions they may have at this stage
  3. Process: The actions they are likely to take to meet their goals
  4. Feeling: Overall feeling throughout this part of the journey
  5. Pain Points: Points where they might get stuck or frustrated
  6. Opportunities: Ideas for addressing the questions they have and/or the anticipated pain points

With these ideas, we intended to start with copywriting the content for various materials, such as the website and emails for mailing lists.

Post-Mortem

Due to the uncertainties around COVID-19, the workshop was cancelled.

But what we learned through the process would still be applicable to future events.

Key Takeaways from User Research for Future Events

Plan speakers in advance to give attendees as much info in the beginning to help them make their decisions

Consider hybrid events for those who don't want to travel

Follow the Nielsen Norman Group tips for copywriting and guiding viewers to the info they need

Even though the ABC Workshop was cancelled, its sister event, the International Breast Density & Cancer Risk Assessment Workshop (IBDW), is still expected to be put on live within the next two years. With a longer history, past participants have already been asking about it in 2022 rather than 2023, when it would've been.
